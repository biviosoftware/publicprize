Boulder's Own Startup Competition

The Boulder Chamber Esprit Venture Challenge is a community-oriented, friendly startup competition with a twist. Everyone gets a say in who wins.

You get to back innovation in Boulder by voting with your dollars. The more you give, the higher the contestant's score. Most importantly, the startups get to keep your contributions so all contestants win!

Why get involved?

Fuse at the Riverside What does it mean to love Boulder? It means getting involved so we have an even better city, because you are part of it. We are a community like no other, from our love of the outdoors to innovation.

You know Boulder is special. We have more scientists, athletes, and entpreneurs per capita than any other city in the U.S. Inc. magazine recently named Boulder America's Startup Capital.

Startups stoke Boulder's economy and keep Boulder at the forefront of innovation. From environmental technology to business process intelligence, Boulder's entrepreneurs are solving the world's problems, one startup at a time.

You can help increase the pace of innovation by contributing to Boulder startups. The Esprit Venture Challenge is showcasing Boulder startups so you get to choose which one to back with your hard-earned dollars. Think of how it would feel to be behind green energy solutions or the next Apple, Facebook, or Google!

For the price of a movie admission, you will help a Boulder entrepreneur succeed, and maybe even be the winner of the Boulder Chamber Esprit Venture Challenge!

How does it work?

Impact Hub Boulder The Esprit Venture Challenge is for seed-stage businesses, specifically, startups with a combined revenue and capital of less than $1,000,000. Contestants submit a brief description, a video, a slide presentation, and founder bios. Once approved, these entries appear on the contest site on their own page as well as in a randomized list of all contestants.

The contest combines crowdfunding and expert input. A panel of judges will rate each contestant individually according to a fixed set of criteria. The judges will also give the startups valuable expert feedback about their business idea, presentation, etc. The judges' score accounts for 60% of the contestant's total score.

The startups are also competing for community backing in a fair and open way. The crowdfunding counts up to 40% towards their total score. The calculation works like this: the startup with the most money raised gets the top score. The other contestants then get a score relative to the top startup. For example, if the top startup raises $60,000 and another startup raises $45,000, then top startup gets the full 40%, and the other startup gets 30% ($45,000 divide by $60,000 times 40%).

Startups have till midnight on September 28, 2014 to submit their entries. You have till midnight on October 21, 2014 to back your favorite startup.
